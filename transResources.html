Trans Resources

Basics

  • Transgender FAQ
    Basics about the term "transgender", gender identity, pronouns, and allyship.

  • Trans intro playlist
    Playlist of videos to give an introduction to trans lives, terminology, and issues.

  • LGBTQ+ definitions
    Trans Student Educational Resources list of LGBTQ+ definitions.

  • PFLAG national glossary of terms
    An extensive list of LGBTQ+ terminology.

  • My pronouns
    Reference site for personal pronouns: why they are important, how to use them, how to handle mistakes, and how to share your own pronouns.

  • Stop using phony science to justify transphobia
    Explanation to show that sex is anything but binary.

  • What does it mean to identify as non-binary?
    Explains what non-binary is, gender as a spectrum, non-binary gender identities, pronouns, and gender-neutral terms.

Allyship

  • Tips for allies of transgender people
    Addresses common misconceptions and mistakes made by transgender allies. It addresses backhanded compliments, inappropriate questions, transitions, and how to make your spaces truly trans-inclusive.

  • 8 things transgender people do not owe you
    Addresses some common pitfalls commonly made my cisgender people when talking to transgender people.

  • 7 tired phrases that marginalize trans people – And What to Use Instead
    Addresses common language mistakes that many well-intentioned people make in regards to trans people.

  • Is your trans allyship half-baked? Here are 6 mistakes that trans allies are still making
    Addresses common mistakes made by allies too transgender people, such as unintentionally transphobic compliments and binary enforcing language.

  • These 7 assumptions about non-binary folks may be hindering your trans allyship
    Addresses common mistakes made by allies in regards to non-binary people.

  • 130+ examples Of cis privilege in all areas of life for you to reflect on and address
    A great reflection piece for both transgender and cisgender people. Prompts cisgender people think about their privilege and to be a better ally. Provides validation to transgender people on their experiences with marginalization.

Parent Resources

  • 4 ways parents can process their feelings after their kid comes out as transgender
    Explains the best way to deal with your feelings (as a parent) when your child comes out as trans. It is important to know how to deal with your feelings so they do not get directed to your child.

  • 6 signs that you might not really respect your transgender loved ones
    A common problem for trans people is having loved ones who want to be supportive, but exhibit unsupportive behaviors. If a loved one in your life recently came out as trans, you likely (to no fault of your own) are exhibiting some of these behaviors.

  • Life Outside the Binary
    A non-binary guide for parents: what that means, and how to begin understanding and supporting your child.

Trans Support

  • The Trevor Project
    The leading national organization providing crisis intervention and suicide prevention services to lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer & questioning (LGBTQ) young people under 25.

  • PFLAG
    PFLAG has many resources: support, information, tools, etc. There are PFLAG chapters all over the United States where transgender or gender-nonconforming individuals and their families can get support.

  • Pronoun Dressing room
    A site where you can test out new pronouns and names.

  • 6 affirmations for trans folks who don’t feel ‘trans enough’

  • transathlete
    Resource for students, athletes, coaches, and administrators to find information about trans inclusion in athletics at various levels of play.

Trans Friendly Internet and Literature

  • Shinigami Eyes
    A browser addon that highlights transphobic and trans-friendly social network pages and users with different colors.

  • Trans Positive People
    A list of influencers and other internet users to add trans positivity to your feeds.

  • Get out of your Head
    A list of resources you can use to debunk transphobia.
    WARNING: This page provides exposure to highly manipulative anti-trans rhetoric. My target audience for this page is those who already had exposure to extremely transphobic rhetoric and are struggling to get it out of their heads.

Physical Transition

  • A physical transition is by no means a requirement to be transgender, but many transgender people may pursue physical treatment options such as HRT (hormone replacement therapy) and surgeries to relieve gender dysphoria.

  • Guide2Transition
    Contains information on feminizing and masculinizing hormones and their effects, complete with great visuals!

  • Planned Parenthood
    A list of Planned Parenthood locations, many of which offer hormone care for transgender patients.

  • Clinics
    A list of clinics which specialize in trans health.

  • Fenway Health Transgender health page
    Contains information about the effects of pronouns and how to access them.
